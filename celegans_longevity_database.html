Why C. elegans

The gold standard for in vivo longevity screening

No other model organism combines biological depth, experimental throughput, and validated longevity science the way C. elegans does. This is where longevity research begins.

🧬
60–80% human gene homology
Nearly all core aging pathways — FOXO, mTOR, AMPK, sirtuins, autophagy — are functionally conserved. Findings in C. elegans consistently predict mammalian outcomes.
⚡
21-day lifespan enables rapid iteration
Full lifespan experiments complete in 3–4 weeks. Rodent studies take 2–3 years. C. elegans delivers statistically powered results at a fraction of the time and cost.
🔬
Transparent body — direct biomarker observation
Fluorescent reporters for DAF-16 nuclear localization, mitochondrial morphology, protein aggregation, and lipid accumulation can all be directly visualized in living animals.
🏆
Four Nobel Prizes — validated by science
Programmed cell death (2002), RNA interference (2006), GFP (2008), and microRNA discovery (2024) were all discovered or validated using C. elegans. The first longevity genes (age-1, daf-2) were identified here.
📊
Whole-organism transcriptomics (BioSēq™)
Unlike cell-based assays, C. elegans captures systems-level responses including inter-tissue signaling, metabolic remodeling, and tissue-specific aging in a complete living organism.

The 12 Hallmarks of Aging

9 of the 12 hallmarks identified in the landmark López-Otín framework are directly conserved and experimentally measurable in C. elegans. Three hallmarks — telomere attrition, cellular senescence, and stem cell exhaustion — have partial or indirect representation.
