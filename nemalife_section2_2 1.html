Who we serve

We exist because the ingredient industry has hard, unsolved problems.

Every engagement starts with a blank-page problem. Here are the five we solve most often — and how we solve them.

Problem 01
"We can't prove our ingredient actually works in vivo."
In vitro assays and cell screens don't reflect the complexity of a living organism. NemaLife's Discovery Flywheel™ delivers real whole-organism efficacy data — across six health domains — in weeks, not years.
Problem 02
"Our ingredient has one claim. We know it can do more."
Most ingredients are under-characterised. We systematically screen for new health signals and map mechanism — turning a single-benefit ingredient into a multi-claim, defensible asset. See: Humiome® Post LB with DSM-Firmenich.
Problem 03
"We're about to invest in a clinical. We need to de-risk it first."
Clinical studies are expensive and long. Our BioSeq™ transcriptomics platform identifies the human-relevant pathways and biomarkers your ingredient activates — giving you the mode-of-action evidence to design a focused, efficient RCT.
Problem 04
"Our competitor just launched. We need differentiation, fast."
Head-to-head comparison studies in C. elegans produce side-by-side efficacy data in under 8 weeks. We've helped partners build an IP position and a stronger science story faster than any other pre-clinical approach.
Problem 05
"We have a promising extract but no IP angle."
Nine partner patents have been enabled through our platform. By uncovering a novel mechanism of action or a new health indication, we give IP teams the novelty they need. Discovery can begin in as little as 4 months.
Problem 06
"We want a synergistic combo — but don't know where to start."
Combination design without biology is guesswork. We screen combinations for synergistic effects across multiple health domains simultaneously, then map the shared mechanisms — like the autophagy pathway shared by thymol, urolithin A, and quercetin.

Nestlé Health Science × NemaLife
Herb-derived terpenoids that activate autophagy — published in Nature Aging
Published · Nature Aging Healthy Aging Autophagy · Mitophagy
+
The Question
Can thymol (thyme) and carvacrol (oregano) — food-grade molecules consumed for centuries — activate the cell's recycling systems in a way that's relevant to healthy aging? And can we map the mechanism well enough to position them against caloric restriction and intermittent fasting pathways?
What NemaLife Did
Using our Bio-Intelligence™ platform, NemaLife screened thymol and carvacrol in C. elegans, revealing activation of autophagy and mitophagy pathways with cross-species signals for healthy aging — showing both compounds sit on the same autophagy map as urolithin A, spermidine, sulforaphane, and quercetin.
The Outcome
The study was published in Nature Aging as part of a global multi-institutional consortium. NemaLife co-authored the mechanistic sections. The findings add food-grade molecules to the growing landscape of ingredients that may target hallmarks-of-aging pathways — opening major new claim territory for Nestlé Health Science.

Medifast × NemaLife
MetaVantage™ — a novel 3-ingredient metabolic combination, validated in vivo
ASN Abstract Metabolic Health PPAR · Fatty Acid Oxidation
+
The Question
Choline, chromium picolinate, and the BPL1™ postbiotic have each independently shown metabolic benefits — but their combination, MetaVantage™, had never been characterised in vivo. Medifast needed functional and mechanistic data across multiple health outcomes, under both healthy and metabolically stressed conditions, to support product launch and future clinical investment.
What NemaLife Did
NemaLife ran MetaVantage™ through the full Discovery Flywheel™ + BioSeq™ workflow under standard and high-sugar diet conditions. Phenotypic outcomes including lifespan, locomotor activity, and metabolic resilience were quantified. Transcriptomics revealed PPAR-associated fatty acid β-oxidation, reduced Notch signalling, and upregulated antioxidant and DNA repair pathways — with all findings submitted as an ASN abstract.
The Outcome
MetaVantage™ demonstrated multiple health benefits in vivo: metabolic resilience, muscle performance, healthy aging, and stress resilience — validated across three diet conditions. The mechanistic data links to conserved human pathways in lipid metabolism and cellular defence, supporting both the product claims and a roadmap to human translational studies.

PLT Health Solutions × NemaLife
A novel green tea extract — gut barrier, longevity, and a pathway to peer review
ASN Abstract · Nutrition 2026 Gut Health · Longevity Catechin Dimers · Epithelial Pathways
+
The Question
PLT Health Solutions had developed a novel standardised green tea extract (NGTE) enriched in galloylated catechin dimers — a structurally complex class of polyphenols with antioxidant properties exceeding those of standard monomeric catechins. The question: could this extract deliver measurable gut barrier and longevity benefits in vivo, and what were the underlying mechanisms?
What NemaLife Did
NemaLife ran NGTE through the full Bio-Intelligence™ platform using the Discovery Flywheel™ framework. Phenotypic outcomes were assessed across lifespan and intestinal barrier integrity using a dye-exclusion assay. BioSeq™ transcriptomics then mapped the mechanistic underpinnings — revealing modulation of redox regulation, epithelial maintenance, and neuromodulation pathways, with gene clusters linked to epithelial function and cellular stress responses.
The Outcome
NGTE improved intestinal barrier integrity by 17.8% vs placebo (p=0.0182) and extended median lifespan by 9.1% (p=0.0014). The work was submitted as an abstract to Nutrition 2026 (ASN) — authored by PLT Health Solutions and NemaLife co-investigators — establishing a pathway to peer-reviewed publication and a defensible mechanistic story for this novel ingredient.
